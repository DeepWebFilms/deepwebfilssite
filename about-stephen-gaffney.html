About Stephen Gaffney

Stephen Gaffney is an Irish filmmaker known for his raw, unapologetic style and fearless exploration of dark subject matter. From micro-budget horror beginnings to working on high-end Netflix productions, his voice remains uniquely personal and provocatively political.

Gaffney wrote and directed the underground hit Red Room (2017), the hard-hitting social drama Bully (2021), and the heroin-fueled crime thriller Class A (2023). His films are characterized by their ultra-realistic tone, emotionally charged performances, and unflinching depiction of violence, addiction, and psychological trauma.

Outside of narrative features, he has directed impactful true crime documentaries such as The Kinahan Cartel, and served as Supervising Editor on the global Netflix docuseries McGregor Forever (2023).

In addition to filmmaking, Gaffney is the founder of Deep Web Films, a boutique horror distribution label championing independent and underground voices. He actively attends and distributes films at major film markets, including the Cannes Film Festival, Berlin’s European Film Market, and other international showcases.

With a growing international profile, Gaffney continues to develop projects that confront, disturb, and provoke — marking him as one of the boldest and most authentic voices in modern Irish cinema.
